### **Saturday (Shopping Day - 1.5-2 hours)**

**Step 5: Execute Shopping Trip**

**Optimal Shopping Time:**
- **After 7 PM:** Bakery items 50% off
- **Late afternoon/evening:** Yellow sticker meat markdowns

**Shopping Strategy (In Order):**

**1. Proteins First (Check for Markdowns)**
- Look for yellow sticker specials (30-50% off)
- Manager's specials (near expiration, safe to freeze)
- BOGO deals on chicken/ground beef/pork
- Purchase and freeze extras if price justified

**2. Produce**
- Buy only what's on meal plan
- Check for manager's specials on slightly imperfect produce
- Avoid pre-cut (more expensive, less savings)

**3. Pantry Staples**
- Focus on BOGO deals
- Stock up on non-perishables when on sale
- Use digital coupons

**4. Dairy/Eggs**
- Check dates carefully
- BOGO cheese is usually good value
- Load up if egg prices are low

**5. Frozen**
- Vegetables (often cheaper than fresh, same nutrition)
- Backup proteins if on sale

**6. Bakery (After 7 PM)**
- 50% off stickers applied automatically
- Bread, rolls, tortillas for week
- Freeze extras

**7. Pet Food (If Monthly Restock)**
- Check for BOGO or sales
- Manufacturer coupons often available
- Buy in bulk if storage allows

**Checkout Process:**
1. Verify digital coupons applied automatically
2. Check receipt for expected savings
3. Save receipt (need for Ibotta/Fetch)

### **Sunday (Meal Prep Day - 2.5-3 hours)**

**Step 7: Execute Meal Prep from Shopping Results**

**Meal Prep Schedule (Optimized Order):**

**Setup (10 minutes):**
- Clear all counter space
- Get out all containers for storage
- Review meal prep checklist
- Set timer for each task

---

**Task 1: Batch Cook Grains (30 minutes - Start First)**
**Why first:** Longest passive cooking time

**Process:**
- Cook rice (white, brown, or mix)
- Cook quinoa if needed
- Cook pasta if needed for week

**Storage:**
- Portion into containers (5 servings each)
- Label with contents and date
- Refrigerate (lasts 5-7 days)

**Meals enabled:** Quick weeknight stir-fries, burrito bowls, pasta dishes

---

**Task 2: Prep Vegetables (45 minutes - While grains cook)**

**Washing:**
- All produce gets washed
- Spin dry leafy greens
- Pat dry other vegetables

**Chopping:**
- Onions: Dice, store in airtight container
- Peppers: Dice or slice, store separately
- Carrots: Peel and slice/dice
- Celery: Chop
- Garlic: Peel and mince (or leave whole if using within 3 days)
- Broccoli/Cauliflower: Cut into florets
- Lettuce: Wash, dry, store in container with paper towel

**Storage:**
- Glass or plastic containers
- Layer paper towels to absorb moisture
- Label with vegetable type
- Refrigerate

**Meals enabled:** All weeknight meals become 30-minute meals

---

**Task 3: Marinate Proteins (15 minutes)**

**Process:**
1. Review week's protein needs
2. Prepare marinades for relevant meals
3. Add proteins to marinades
4. Store in refrigerator or freezer

**Examples:**
- Monday chicken: Italian herb marinade
- Wednesday pork: Teriyaki marinade
- Friday fish: Lemon-garlic marinade

**Storage:**
- Ziplock bags or covered containers
- Label with day of week and meal
- Refrigerate if using within 2 days
- Freeze if using later in week

**Meals enabled:** Flavorful proteins, minimal weeknight prep

---

**Task 4: Prep Breakfasts (30 minutes)**

**Option A: Overnight Oats (5 days)**
- Portion oats into 5 jars
- Add milk, chia seeds, sweetener
- Top with fruit before eating
- Refrigerate

**Option B: Egg Muffins (12 servings)**
- Whisk eggs with vegetables, cheese
- Pour into muffin tins
- Bake 20 minutes
- Cool and store
- Reheat 30 seconds

**Option C: Make-Ahead Smoothie Packs**
- Portion fruits into bags
- Add spinach, protein powder
- Freeze
- Blend with liquid each morning

**Storage:**
- Refrigerate 5-7 days
- Or freeze and thaw as needed

**Meals enabled:** Grab-and-go breakfasts, no morning stress

---

**Task 5: Prep Lunches (30 minutes)**

**Strategy:** Cook once, eat 5 times

**Examples:**
- **Grain bowls:** Grain base + protein + roasted veggies + sauce
- **Salads:** Greens + protein + toppings (keep dressing separate)
- **Soups:** Batch cook Sunday, portion into containers
- **Sandwiches/Wraps:** Prep fillings, assemble fresh daily

**Assembly:**
- 5 identical containers (Monday-Friday)
- OR 2-3 different options to rotate
- Label with day and contents

**Storage:**
- Refrigerate all
- Dressing in separate small containers

**Meals enabled:** Healthy lunches, no takeout needed

---

**Task 6: Cook Freezer Meals (30 minutes - Optional)**

**When to do this:**
- Ground beef/chicken on sale
- Extra time available
- Want backup meals

**Examples:**
- Chili (freezes perfectly)
- Taco meat (portion and freeze)
- Meatballs (cook and freeze)
- Casseroles (assemble, freeze unbaked)

**Storage:**
- Freeze in meal-sized portions
- Label with contents, date, reheat instructions
- Use within 3 months

**Meals enabled:** Emergency dinners, backup for busy weeks

---

**Cleanup (15 minutes):**
- Wash all prep dishes
- Wipe down counters
- Organize fridge with prepped items
- Review week's meal plan one more time

## üí∞ SAVINGS OPTIMIZATION STRATEGIES

### **BOGO Maximization**

**Florida BOGO Rules Reminder:**
- Must buy both items
- Each rings at 50% off
- Can use coupons on BOTH items

**Stacking Example:**
```
Item: Pasta sauce (BOGO)
Regular: $3.49 each
BOGO: $1.75 each √ó 2 = $3.50

Coupons available:
- Manufacturer: $0.75 off 1
- Publix digital: $0.50 off 1
- Ibotta: $0.25 back on 1

Strategy:
Buy 2 jars
Use manufacturer coupon on each: $0.75 √ó 2 = -$1.50
Use digital coupon on one: -$0.50
Ibotta on one: -$0.25

Final: $3.50 - $1.50 - $0.50 = $1.50 + get $0.25 back
TOTAL: $1.25 for 2 jars
Regular would be: $6.98
SAVINGS: $5.73 (82%)
```

**Best BOGO Items to Stock:**
- Non-perishables (canned goods, pasta, sauce)
- Frozen proteins (freeze for future)
- Pantry staples (oils, spices, baking)

---

### **Yellow Sticker Strategy**

**What are yellow stickers?**
- Manager markdowns on items nearing sell-by date
- Typically 30-50% off
- Safe to use if consumed soon or frozen

**When to find them:**
- Early morning (6-8 AM): Previous day's markdowns
- Late afternoon (4-6 PM): Today's markdowns
- Evening (after 7 PM): Final markdowns

**What to buy:**
- Proteins: Freeze immediately if not using within 2 days
- Produce: Use within 2-3 days
- Bakery: Freeze bread/buns
- Dairy: Check dates carefully

**What to avoid:**
- Items past sell-by date
- Items with off smell/appearance
- Dairy expiring next day (unless using immediately)

---

### **Manager's Special Strategy**

**What are manager's specials?**
- Not marked down yet, but special pricing
- Often unadvertised
- Found throughout store

**Where to look:**
- Meat department (ask butcher)
- Produce section (bulk deals)
- Bakery (day-old section)
- End caps (clearance items)

**When to buy:**
- Price is 40%+ off regular
- Can use immediately or freeze
- Matches meal plan or can adjust plan

---

### **After 7 PM Bakery Discount**

**Automatic 50% off stickers:**
- Applied to bakery items after 7 PM
- Fresh bread, rolls, bagels, muffins
- Cookies, cakes (if not custom)

**Strategy:**
- Shop after 7 PM on Saturdays
- Buy bread/buns for week
- Freeze extras
- Buy treats at half price

**Best buys:**
- Sandwich bread (freeze half)
- Hamburger/hot dog buns (freeze)
- Bagels (freeze)
- Tortillas (don't freeze well, buy only what you'll use)

---

### **Monthly Pet Food Strategy**

**Budget:** $80-105/month for 6 pets (5 cats, 1 dog)

**Optimization:**
1. Buy during BOGO weeks only
2. Stock up for 2-3 months when on sale
3. Use manufacturer coupons (often available)
4. Check Ibotta for pet food offers
5. Buy largest bags if storage allows (better per-pound price)

**Never compromise on:**
- Food quality for price
- Veterinary recommendations
- Known allergies/sensitivities
